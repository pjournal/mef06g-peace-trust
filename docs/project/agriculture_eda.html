Increase in average temperature in Turkey in 2018, also coincides with the decrease in Agriculrural area. Note that we need further statistical tests to show the relation between, this presentations only shows the raw data. However, *there are evidences suggesting that rising temperatures due to climate change can have negative impacts on agriculture, including crop yields and the productivity of livestock.

*Higher temperatures can lead to more frequent heatwaves, which can stress plants and animals and reduce their growth and productivity.

*In addition, higher temperatures can also lead to more frequent and severe droughts, which can reduce crop yields and increase the risk of wildfires.

*Higher temperatures can also create conditions that are more favorable for pests and diseases, which can further reduce crop yields.

There are many resources about the relation between agricultural area loss and higher temperatures:

*The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) is an international body that assesses the science related to climate change. The IPCC’s Fifth Assessment Report, which was published in 2013, includes a chapter on “Agriculture, Forestry and Other Land Use,” which discusses the impacts of climate change on agriculture. You can find the report here: https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar5/wg2/

*The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) has a Climate Hubs program that aims to provide farmers, ranchers, and forest landowners with information and tools to help them adapt to the impacts of climate change. The USDA’s Climate Hubs website includes a section on “Agriculture and Climate Change,” which discusses the impacts of rising temperatures on agriculture in the United States: https://www.climatehubs.oce.usda.gov/topics/agriculture-and-climate-change

*The United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) is an organization that works to improve the food security and nutrition of people around the world. The FAO’s website includes a section on “Climate Change and Agriculture,” which discusses the impacts of rising temperatures on agriculture and offers resources and information for farmers and other stakeholders: http://www.fao.org/climatechange/en/

CO2 emission is also an important metric for measuring the climate change. CO2 emissions (metric tons per capita) Carbon dioxide emissions are those stemming from the burning of fossil fuels and the manufacture of cement. They include carbon dioxide produced during consumption of solid, liquid, and gas fuels and gas flaring. WorldBank launches the CO2 emissions (metric tons per capita) data for every country. I used WorldBank

Note that, Data consists of the CO2 emission for 2009-2019

Reducing CO2 emissions from agriculture can help mitigate the impacts of climate change and reduce the risk of agricultural loss.
